# Prompt

Why is the sky blue?

# Response

The sky is blue due to a phenomenon called **Rayleigh scattering**. Here’s a breakdown of why it happens:

* **Sunlight is made up of all the colors of the rainbow.** When sunlight enters the Earth's atmosphere, it bumps into tiny air molecules (mostly nitrogen and oxygen).

* **Blue light scatters more than other colors.** Rayleigh scattering is the scattering of electromagnetic radiation (like light) by particles of a much smaller wavelength. Blue and violet light have shorter wavelengths than other colors like red and orange.  This means blue light is scattered much more strongly by the air molecules.

* **We see the scattered blue light.**  Because blue light is scattered so much, it’s spread all over the sky, making it appear blue to our eyes.

**Think of it like this:** Imagine throwing a handful of small marbles (blue light) and a handful of larger rocks (red light) into a pool of water. The small marbles will bounce around much more easily than the large rocks.

**Why not violet?** Violet light is scattered even more than blue light, but:

* **The sun emits less violet light.**
* **Our eyes are less sensitive to violet.**

**So, in summary, the sky is blue because blue light is scattered more by the atmosphere than other colors, making it appear to fill the sky.**

**Want to learn more? Here's a simple analogy:**

Imagine throwing a handful of small bouncy balls (blue light) and a handful of large, heavy balls (red light) into a trampoline. The bouncy balls will bounce all over the place, while the heavy balls mostly just roll along.
